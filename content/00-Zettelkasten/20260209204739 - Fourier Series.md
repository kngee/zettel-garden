- Any periodic waveform can be represented as the sum of an infinite number of sinusoid and cosinusoidal components and a constant term.
$$f(t)=a_0+\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}a_ncos(n\omega t)+\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}b_nsin(n\omega t)$$
- $a_0$ is represented by the time average of $f(t)$ taken over one period which we can consider to be the DC voltage level.
- We enjoy the benefits of the exponential notation that allows us to express the summation as a single term
$$f(t)=\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty}d_ne^{jn\omega t}$$
