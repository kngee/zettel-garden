In our study of telecommunications it is shown that if continuous in time signals can be assumed to be a special case vector, several vector space operations and definitions can be used to our advantage.

Let us define a vector $\bar{g}$ which N samples of time domain signals that are discrete in time
$$\bar{g} = \begin{pmatrix}g(t_1) & g(t_2) & \cdots & g(t_N)\end{pmatrix}$$
By making use of the following limit to infinity $$\lim_{N\rightarrow\infty}\bar{g}=g(t)~~~t\in[a,b]$$
Therefore, we have shown that a signal continuous in time is a special case of a standard vector. Now we get to go very far with this basic representation.
