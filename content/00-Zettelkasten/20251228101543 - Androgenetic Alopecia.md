- There are 7 stages from which it is clear that I am currently going through stage 3 and approaching stage 4.
- Male pattern baldness causes the small depressions in your scalp at the base of your hairs ([hair follicles](https://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/body/23435-hair-follicle)) to gradually shrink, often in a specific pattern. As your hair follicles shrink, your individual hairs get thinner and shorter. Over time, those hairs stop growing at all. (*this is exactly what is happening to me*)
- Main cause in my case: [Dihydrotestosterone (DHT)](https://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/articles/24555-dht-dihydrotestosterone) is a type of [androgen](https://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/articles/22002-androgens). Androgens are a group of sex hormones that help people enter puberty and mature physically. Physical developments include hair growth on the face, scalp, chest, underarms and genitals. Medical experts and researchers think that there might be a link between DHT and your hair follicles shrinking.
