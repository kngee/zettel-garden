When trying to find the magnitude component of some vector $\bar{g}$ along another vector $\bar{x}$. We can quantify this projection using a scalar $c$ that scales the magnitude of $\bar{x}$. 

Using cosine, we create the following equation, $$c\parallel x\parallel = \parallel{g}\parallel cos\theta$$
Multiplying both sides by the magnitude of x results in an equation known as the inner product of two real valued vectors. $$<g,x>=\parallel x\parallel\parallel{g}\parallel cos\theta$$
The inner product represented above has a value of 0 when the vectors are **orthogonal**.
