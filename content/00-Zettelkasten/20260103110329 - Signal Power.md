Like Signal Energy, Signal Power is a quantity that we use to measure the strength of a signal. The reason for its creation are for signals that do not produce a meaningful energy result since they do not converge to zero. 

The Signal Power is takes advantage of observed periodic behaviour or any statistical regularity and create an average power per some time unit. A signal is said to be a Power Signal if the mean square power of the signal is non-zero but also finite.

$$0<\lim_{T\rightarrow\infty}\frac{1}{T}\int_{\frac{T}{2}}^{\frac{T}{2}}|g(t)|^2dt<\infty$$
Not all signals that aren't Signal Energy are necessarily Energy Signals. Remember that the key criteria is some form of periodicity or regularity.
