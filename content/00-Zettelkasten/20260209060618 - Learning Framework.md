> The premise of this system is to use the concepts of chunking and the diffuse mode to commit more of what I learn to my long term memory and understanding. This involves changing my otherwise poor habit of leaving things for quite late. Therefore, I need a system that fights this and gets me working on time. 

- The system starts with prereading through the textbook/lecture and captures ideas that aren't yet in my own words. The only thing i try to do at this stage is capture what is given to me in the structure that it was given to me. I can maybe tag it but that is the extent at this stage.
- The second stage will then try to consolidate the information that was captured into my own words and grouped no longer in the provided structure but now by the core idea extracted from the broader note. This goes for any derivations or proofs that I need to know to make an assumption.
- The third stage spams exercises and problems that will help commit that which I have put into my own words into confidence and hopefully more that I can add to the stage 2 notes.
- Finally, a consolidating short essay that I should be able to write without any aid hat describes why I've learnt what I have and my opinions because the more opinionated I become about what I learn, the more likely it is to stick in my memory.

> The difficult part of this system is figuring out a timing for how much time needs to be spent on a stage

- Stage 1: As close as possible to when I read the material for the first time or when I am hearing it during a lecture
- Stage 2: In a physical notebook (either daily/weekly) write out the new *fleeting* experiences we have accumulated in the last few days as a record of what was done. ***However, we can just mark fleeting notes and reviewed after we have gone through them***
- Stage 3: Review the fleeting notes once more and if the idea stands out, turn the concept into a permanent note that focuses on a single idea trying to connect that with the other ideas that we have already learnt. Has to be in our own words and cannot consult the textbook while building this up.
- Stage 4: Focus on doing problems during the week and summarising the lessons we get out of them through consolidated notes. Cannot refer to anything but our own permanent notes.
- Stage 5: In an essay straight from the dome, summarise the relevance of the chapter, your understand before and after taking it and what you think about the material. Just tell a complete story of your understanding.
