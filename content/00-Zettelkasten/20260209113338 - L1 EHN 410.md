- Cryptographic algorithms can be grouped into 4 main categories
- Computer Security is defined as "the protection afforded to an automated information system in order to attain the applicable objectives of reserving the integrity, availability and confidentiality of information system resource
- The 3 computer security objectives are confidentiality, integrity and availability.
- There are 5 essential network and computer security requirements.
- There are 3 levels of impact when considering Breach of Security.
	- High, Moderate and Low
- There are many computer security challenges.
- OSI Security Architecture describes the differences between security- attack, mechanism and service
- Threats vs Attacks: A potential vulnerability vs An intelligent verified attempt to breach.
- Passive vs Active Attacks: Passive attacks learn information without affecting system resources while active attacks affect the resources/operation.
	- Passive attacks take the form of eavesdropping / monitoring 
	- The 2 types of passive attacks are the **release of message contents** and **traffic analysis**
	- Active attacks create some sort of false data stream, a system is to detect these attacks and recover from any disruption they cause.
	- There are 4 kinds of active attacks Masquerade, Replay, Modification of messages, Denial of Service
