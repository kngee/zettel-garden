- Traditionally, the primary metric for performance in telecom evolution is downlink speed. That is now changing to uplink speed as we more and more devices, particularly those internet of things devices, prioritise uplink functionality.
- Progress is being made on closing the *Terahertz gap*.
- Hollowing out Fiber speeds it up. Could potentially be used for extremely low latency applications like finance tech and sensor networks.
- There are potentially acoustic sensing capabilities in existing Fiber networks for events such as earthquake alerts, large spacecraft landings etc.
- No one has solved the quantum signal repeater or quantitum signal amplifier problem yet. The first customers for this will be governments and financial institutions that require quantum secure, extremely low latency communications for their supercomputers.
